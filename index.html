Anandarup Mukherjee

Ph.D., FRSS, SMIEEE, MIET

Senior Research Associate, University of Cambridge, U.K.

Democratising Digital Transformation, Digital Twins, Complex Networks, and Industry 4.0

Career Summary

I am an experienced researcher with over 14 years of R&D expertise, more than 6 years of team leadership, and a versatile skill set spanning hardware and software domains. My work has led to award-winning IoT-based technology prototypes and a deep understanding of IoT architectures.

I specialize in developing smart and aware sensing systems that intersect industrial automation and asset management. My research interests include low-cost digitalization and transformation architectures, UAV networks and swarms, industrial automation and communication, and applied machine learning.

In my professional roles, I contribute as the Vice-Chair of IEEE P1954 - Standard for Self-Organizing Spectrum-Agile Unmanned Aerial Vehicle Communications, Associate Editor for Springer Nature’s "Peer-to-Peer Networking and Applications" journal, and Area Expert in IoT Communications and Interoperability within IEEE ComSoc’s Special Interest Group on IoT for e-Health.

  • Current Role: Senior Research Associate, Institute for Manufacturing (IfM), Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge, U.K.
  • Key Metrics: 1300+ citations, h-index: 22, i-10 index: 33.
  • Achievements: Founding Organizing Chair of Low-Cost Digital Solutions for Industrial Automation (LoDiSA 23 & 24) workshops - a pioneering global scientific forum for formalizing research on low-cost technologies.
  • Research Areas: Digital Twins, Complex Networks and Systems, Interoperability, Industrial IoT, Condition Monitoring, Prognostics, e-health, UAV Networks, Low-cost Sensing, Machine Learning.
